### Social Capital in the United Kingdom: Evidence from Six Billion Friendships [[Paper](https://osf.io/preprints/socarxiv/kb7dy_v1)]

With Thomas Rutter, Shankar Iyer et al. (2025)

<details>
<summary>Click for abstract</summary>
<br>
This paper measures social capital across the United Kingdom using anonymized Facebook data covering six billion friendships. We construct novel measures of social connectedness and economic connectedness at granular geographic levels and examine their relationship with economic mobility and other outcomes.
</details>

### Who Responds to Climate Risk? Disasters, Insurance Prices, and the Sorting of Migrants by Income

This project examines whether Americans migrate away from climate-risky areas and how migration responses vary by income level. Using IRS migration data combined with FEMA risk data and insurance premiums, I find that higher-income migration corridors move away from rising premiums while lower-income corridors move toward high-premium destinations.
